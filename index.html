Introduction

Beijing is the political, cultural, and educational center of China. With a history of over 3,000 years and serving as the capital for centuries, Beijing blends imperial heritage with modern life.

  • Population: ~21 million (metro)
  • Official language: Mandarin
  • Famous for: Forbidden City, Great Wall, Tiananmen Square, Summer Palace

Top Attractions

Forbidden City

The imperial palace for Ming and Qing dynasties with palaces, halls and gardens.

Great Wall

Iconic defensive wall with scenic hiking sections like Badaling and Mutianyu.

Summer Palace

Imperial gardens with lakes, palaces, and temples, blending nature and architecture beautifully.

Temple of Heaven

A distinctive circular building set in a large park—an important Ming/Qing ritual site.

Things to Do

Hike the Great Wall

Choose Mutianyu for fewer crowds or Jinshanling/Badavling for classic views.

Stroll in hutongs

Explore narrow lanes, courtyard homes and local teahouses around Houhai and Nanluoguxiang.

Shop & Eat at Wangfujing

Street food, malls and souvenir shopping in one lively area.

Local Events

  • Chinese New Year celebrations (late Jan–Feb depending on lunar calendar)
  • Beijing International Music Festival (usually in autumn)
  • Mid-Autumn Festival activities and lantern displays
